<div class="about-overview" markdown="1">

Dr. Mariana Belgiu is an Associate Professor in GeoAI and multi-temporal remote sensing for agri-environmental studies in the Department of Earth Observation Science at the Faculty ITC, University of Twente. Her work connects remote sensing, machine learning, and environmental sciences, with a strong focus on agriculture.

The group develops data-centric AI methods for multi-temporal Earth Observation data, with attention to scarce-label learning, transferability, environmental mapping, crop monitoring, crop quality, yield forecasting, food security, and climate vulnerability. A growing research line explores how Earth Observation and spatial data can help understand hidden hunger by estimating and predicting nutrient levels in crops.

</div>

<section class="home-section" markdown="1">

## Research themes

- **Data-centric AI for Earth Observation:** methods for multi-temporal optical, hyperspectral, and radar imagery.
- **Learning with scarce labels:** robust and transferable algorithms for mapping crops, land cover, and urban environments.
- **Hidden hunger and food security:** remote sensing approaches for estimating crop and soil nutrient indicators.
- **Urban vulnerability and inclusion:** citizen-guided AI and Earth Observation for slum mapping and climate vulnerability assessment.
- **GeoAI skills and education:** curriculum analysis, training material, and inclusive Earth Observation capacity building.

## Teaching and community

Mariana teaches optical remote sensing, image analysis, machine learning, and deep learning at ITC. She coordinates Advanced Image Analysis and contributes to Machine Learning for Geospatial Sciences, Space & Time, internships, and MSc thesis supervision on spatial and Earth Observation data for societal and environmental challenges.

She is active in EO4all, a working group that promotes inclusive Earth Observation science, gender equity, diversity, student intake, and project acquisition through new partnerships. She is also affiliated with the Digital Society Institute and contributes to scientific service through editorial and committee roles.
